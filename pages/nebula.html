What is a Nebula?

A nebula is a giant cloud of dust and gas in space. Some nebulae form stars, while others are remnants of dead or dying stars. They can be large, bright clouds like the famous Orion Nebula or dark, dense regions of space where stars are still forming.

Types of Nebulae

Nebulae can be classified into three main types: emission nebulae, which glow due to the ionization of their gas; reflection nebulae, which shine by reflecting the light of nearby stars; and planetary nebulae, which are shells of gas expelled by dying stars.

Famous Nebulae

Some of the most famous nebulae include the Orion Nebula, a massive star-forming region; the Crab Nebula, a remnant of a supernova explosion; and the Ring Nebula, a glowing shell of gas surrounding a dying star.
