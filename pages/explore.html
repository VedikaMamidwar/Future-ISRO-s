Stars

Learn about the different types of stars and their life cycles.

Nebula

A stunning, vast cloud of gas and dust in space, where stars are born and shine brilliantly.

white dwarf

A dense, small star that remains after a star sheds its outer layers.

black dwarf

A cold, inactive white dwarf that has exhausted its remaining heat and energy.

Constellation

A group of stars forming a recognizable pattern or shape in the night sky.

Black hole

A region in space with extremely strong gravity, where nothing, not even light, can escape.

Milky way

The galaxy that contains our solar system, composed of billions of stars and planets.

cosmic dust

Tiny particles of matter in space, often composed of gas, dust, and other elements.

Astronaut

A trained professional who travels and works in space to conduct scientific research and missions.
