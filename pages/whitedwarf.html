What is a White Dwarf?

A white dwarf is a small, dense star that is the remnants of a star that has exhausted its nuclear fuel and shed its outer layers.

Characteristics

White dwarfs are incredibly dense, with mass comparable to that of the Sun but volume similar to that of Earth.

Life Cycle

After a star becomes a white dwarf, it cools and fades over billions of years, eventually becoming a cold, dark "black dwarf."
