What is Cosmic Dust?

Cosmic dust consists of tiny particles found throughout space, originating from stars, comets, and planets. They play a key role in the formation of new stars and planets.

The Role of Cosmic Dust

Cosmic dust particles help in cooling gas clouds, triggering the collapse that leads to star formation. Without dust, galaxies and solar systems wouldn't exist!

Detecting Cosmic Dust

Space telescopes like Hubble capture the light scattered by cosmic dust. ISRO uses advanced instruments to study these particles and understand the early universe.
