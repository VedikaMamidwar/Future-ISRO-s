What is a Black Dwarf?

A black dwarf is a theoretical stellar remnant that is the final stage of a white dwarf, having cooled to the point where it no longer emits significant heat or light.

Characteristics

Black dwarfs are extremely cold, and since the universe is not old enough for any black dwarfs to exist, their properties are largely theoretical.

Formation

It is theorized that a black dwarf forms after a white dwarf cools for billions of years, eventually reaching a temperature close to absolute zero.
